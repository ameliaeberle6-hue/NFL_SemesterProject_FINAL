Section 01

Literature
Review

Social Media & Sports Organizations

There has been a lot of intrest and focus on studying how professional sports franchises use social media to connect with and expand their fan bases. When we really look into how professional athletes use Twitter to communicate directly with fans, the truth can be revealed. It has been found that authentic and personable content generates a notable increase in engagement over that of promotional material. There has been a foundational principle established that is still relevant to Instagram today: audiences respond to personality and access just as much as (or even more than) performance.

Athletic Performance & Fan Engagement

Several studies have examined this direct relationship between on-field success and fan behavior that we speak of. It has been found that winning percentage is positively connected to game attendance, merchandise sales, and overall media exposure. However, their findings also showed that this effect has variables. For example, teams that are consistently good do not see proportional increases in engagement over time. Uniqueness, whether for better or for worse, and momentum matter just as much as raw winning percentage.

Celebrity Culture & Sports Crossover

An important aspect of sports social media growth that has not been talked about enough is the role of celebrity culture and media crossover. Some studies have introduced the concept of the "sport celebrity" as a cultural commodity, and they have argued that certain athletes transcend their sport to become broader and greater than the sport itself in a sense, dramatically expanding their audience beyond traditional sports fans. Think Travis Kelce, Tom Brady, or Patrick Mahomes... Their Instagram accounts attract millions of followers who are not just interested in football, but in THEM.

This phenomenon has become even more pronounced in the social media era. It has been found that Instagram accounts belonging to athletes who engaged with entertainment, fashion, and lifestyle content attracted significantly larger audiences than those focused purely on athletic content. This is because people follow indivudals not only for their professions, but because they are relateable. This has direct relevance to our study, as teams associated with high-profile celebrities certainly seem to experience follower spikes that have little to do with their win/loss record.

Instagram as a Research Subject

Instagram, launched in 2010 and acquired by Facebook in 2012, has become a main platforms for sports content. Visual platforms are largely well-suited to sports content because they capture the aesthetic and emotional aspects of athletic performance in ways that text simply cannot.

Teams that post more frequently and use more varied content types, such as behind-the-scenes footage, player spotlights, or game highlights, have earned higher follower growth rates than teams that posted only game-day content. This suggests that content strategy often should have variety beyond the game itself to maximize engagement.

What We Still Don't Know

There is a growing body of research on this matter, but several questions remain. Most studies focus on short time windows, such as a single season or a few years. This makes it difficult to identify long-term trends. Few studies have truly tried to seperate the specific contribution of unique external events that go "viral" within our culture (celebrity news, viral moments, controversies, drama, etc) from performance-based growth. Our project contributes to addressing both of these gaps by examining a thirteen-year dataset and intentionally flagging known cultural events as variables in our analysis.
