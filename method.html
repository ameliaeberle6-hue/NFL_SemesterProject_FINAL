Research
Methodology

A step-by-step account of how we collected, organized, and analyzed data to answer our research question: does NFL team performance correlate with Instagram follower growth?

Research Question

Our central question is: Does NFL team performance correlate with Instagram follower growth? This is a quantitative research question — it asks whether two measurable variables move together in a statistically meaningful way. To answer it, we needed two parallel datasets: one tracking each team's regular season win/loss record year by year, and one tracking each team's Instagram follower count over the same period.

Data Collection

  • 01
    NFL Win/Loss Records — Covers.com We collected regular season win totals for all 32 NFL teams from 2012 through 2024 using Covers.com's historical standings pages (e.g., covers.com/sport/football/nfl/standings/2012-2013). For each season, we recorded each team's regular season win total. Data was recorded manually into a shared spreadsheet.
  • 02
    Instagram Follower Counts — Social Blade Instagram follower counts were collected using Social Blade (socialblade.com), a third-party analytics platform that tracks historical social media statistics. For each of the 32 NFL teams, we looked up the official team Instagram account and recorded approximate follower counts at the end of each calendar year from 2012 to 2024.
  • 03
    Cultural Event Annotation In addition to Instagram followers and NFL win/loss records, we also manually made a list off off-field events that could possibly explain outlier follower spikes. these included celebrity relationships with players, Super Bowl wins, and viral media moments. These were flagged in our graphs to provide context.

Scope & Limitations

To keep the project manageable, we made several deliberate boundaries. 1.) We focused only on regular season win totals. 2.) We used year-end follower snapshots rather than tracking follower counts on a weekly or monthly basis. 3.) We limited our analysis to the official team Instagram accounts. This was important to make sure we were measuring team-level brand growth rather than individual athlete popularity.

Analysis Approach

Our analysis is primarily quantitative. We compared win totals to year-over-year follower growth rates for each team and looked for patterns across the league. We also gathered more qualitative data by identifying then researching what off-field factors might explain those anomalies.

Our findings are presented through line graphs and bar charts allowing readers to explore individual team trends and compare teams side by side.
