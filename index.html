Introduction

This project looks into whether NFL team performance connects in any way to Instagram follower growth across all 32 teams from the years 2012 to 2024. As social media has become one of the primary ways sports organizations connect with fans, understanding what drives follower growth has real implications for team branding, marketing strategy, and fan engagement.

Our central research question is: Does NFL team performance correlate with Instagram follower growth? In order to answer this we have collected win and loss data from Covers.com and Instagram follower counts from Social Blade. Then, we analyzed trends across all of these NFL teams over a thirteen-year period.

Our findings may be surprising to some. While sustained success such as the Kansas City Chiefs' back-to-back trips to the Super Bowl does produce an extent of follower growth, some of the most clear upward spikes in our data are tied to these moments that are far greater than the game being played on the field. Whether it be celebrity relationships, high-profile player signings, or viral media coverage, there are certain things on the internet that ultimately trump team success when it comes to the numbers that they are doing on social media. The Dallas Cowboys, historically one of Instagram's most-followed teams, have kept massive audiences through brand power even during losing seasons ("America's Team").

This site presents our full research process. That includes a literature review of prior work on sports and social media, a detailed description of how we went about our data collection, interactive visualizations of our findings, and a conclusion that compares and contrasts our results to the larger conversation about fan engagement in this rapidly evolving digital world.
